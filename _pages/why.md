            <h2 class="about_section_title">Why diversity, specifically</h2>
        </div>
        <div class="row">

            <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-6 mb-30">
                <div class="pl_feature_box">
                    <span class="why_stat_number">80%</span>
                    <p class="pl_feature_sub">Senior leadership leak</p>
                    <p class="pl_feature_body">In McKinsey's 2023 review of 60,000+ private-capital professionals, <strong>80%</strong> of managing-director roles are held by white professionals; women hold just 15% globally. Promotion to principal level – the rung below MD – ran 2.8&times; faster for men than women. Women from ethnic and racial minorities were the least represented group at every level.<sup><a href="#ref-1">1</a></sup></p>
                </div>
            </div>

            <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-6 mb-30">
                <div class="pl_feature_box">
                    <span class="why_stat_number">+121%</span>
                    <p class="pl_feature_sub">More patent citations</p>
                    <p class="pl_feature_body">Research by the Royal Academy of Engineering found that engineering businesses with higher diversity generated <strong>19% more revenue</strong> from new products and services, and saw <strong>121% more patent citations</strong> – evidence that diverse teams drive the kind of innovation output that matters in research-technical work.<sup><a href="#ref-2">2</a>,&nbsp;<a href="#ref-3">3</a></sup></p>
                </div>
            </div>

            <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-6 mb-30">
                <div class="pl_feature_box">
                    <div class="pl_feature_icon"><i class="icon_lightbulb_alt"></i></div>
                    <h3 class="pl_feature_title">The mechanism</h3>
                    <p class="pl_feature_body">Scott Page's mathematical work on group problem-solving shows <em>why</em>: variety in heuristics, perspectives and mental models beats raw individual ability past a threshold. Diverse teams literally search a wider solution space.<sup><a href="#ref-4">4</a></sup></p>
                </div>
            </div>

        </div>

        <div class="row">
            <div class="col-lg-12">
                <p style="margin-top: 20px; padding: 18px 22px; border-left: 3px solid var(--color-brand); background: var(--color-brand-soft); font-size: 15px;">
                    <strong>UK context.</strong> The House of Commons Science, Innovation and Technology Committee's 2023 report <em>Diversity and Inclusion in STEM</em> concluded that significant progress is still needed on representation across the UK STEM workforce, and the Government accepted that case in its formal response. The 2025 BSA / APPG briefing similarly finds that EDI in UK STEM is linked to stronger commercial performance and to talent attraction and retention.<sup><a href="#ref-5">5</a>,&nbsp;<a href="#ref-3">3</a></sup></p>

<!-- ─── Why psych safety ─── -->
<section class="about_overview blog_area why_refs">
    <div class="container">
        <div class="about_section_header">
            <h2 class="about_section_title">Why diversity alone is not enough</h2>
        </div>
        <div class="row align-items-start">

            <div class="col-lg-7 col-md-12">
                <p>Hiring people from underrepresented groups doesn't, on its own, change outcomes. If they don't feel safe to speak, disagree, point out a problem, or admit uncertainty, you have a diverse room and a homogeneous conversation – and the research shows that produces homogeneous results.</p>
                <p>This is what Amy Edmondson at Harvard Business School calls <strong>psychological safety</strong>: the shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking. It is the variable that makes diversity work.<sup><a href="#ref-6">6</a></sup></p>
                <p>Decades of empirical work since – summarised most recently in the CIPD's 2024 evidence review – show that psychologically safe teams display more learning behaviour: they speak up about errors, ask for help, and admit uncertainty rather than hiding it. The result is counter-intuitive: less safe teams may look better on the surface but actually make more mistakes – mistakes that go unreported, and therefore unfixed.<sup><a href="#ref-7">7</a></sup></p>
            </div>

            <div class="col-lg-4 offset-lg-1 col-md-12">
                <div class="pl_feature_box about_meta_card">
                    <p class="pl_feature_sub">Google's Project Aristotle</p>
                    <p class="why_pullquote">A two-year study of 180 Google teams identified five key dynamics that distinguished high-performing teams. <strong>Psychological safety emerged as the most important</strong> – the foundation that allows the other four (dependability, structure &amp; clarity, meaning, impact) to function.<span class="why_pullquote_attr">— Project Aristotle, Google People Operations.<sup><a href="#ref-8">8</a></sup></span></p>
                </div>
            </div>

        </div>
    </div>
</section>
