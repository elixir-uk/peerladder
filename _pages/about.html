Peer Ladder is a peer-mentoring initiative that empowers underrepresented digital Research Technical Professionals (dRTPs) to climb the leadership ladder together – building confidence, community and inclusive leadership across UK research-performing organisations (RPOs).

dRTPs are essential to data-driven research, yet many remain in supporting roles without clear routes into leadership. Women, disabled people, LGBTQIA+, and minority ethnic professionals are particularly under-represented at senior levels, limiting diversity of thought and innovation.

Peer Ladder builds on a proven European model (ELEAD) that successfully enabled women leaders within the European ELIXIR life-science infrastructure. The UK pilot adapts this approach to all underrepresented dRTP groups, focusing on peer mentoring – the most impactful element of the original model.

Participants receive structured peer support and training from Open Life Science (OLS), which provides onboarding and facilitation to create a safe, inclusive learning environment that builds allyship and leadership skills.

As a test-pilot, Peer Ladder will demonstrate the value of this model nationally. Participants will be supported with materials and guidance to continue meeting as a self-organised group beyond the project. Outcomes will feed into DisCouRSE, ELIXIR-UK, and SSI to shape inclusive leadership and EDI policy across the digital research community.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a dRTP?

Digital Research Technical Professionals develop and maintain the digital infrastructure, software, data pipelines and computational tools that power modern research — including research software engineers, data stewards, bioinformaticians, and HPC specialists.

Who can apply?

Anyone with at least three years' experience in a dRTP role at a UK research-performing organisation. PeerLadder particularly welcomes applications from women, disabled individuals, neurodivergent professionals, LGBTQIA+ practitioners, and those from minority ethnic or lower socioeconomic backgrounds.

What is the time commitment?

Typically one to two hours per month outside of workshops. The programme includes a virtual onboarding, one in-person workshop in Manchester (travel fully covered), a systemic leadership workshop, and monthly virtual peer-mentoring sessions from August 2026 through March 2027.

Is there a cost?

No. PeerLadder is fully funded by DisCouRSE. There is no application fee and no charge to participate. All reasonable travel and accommodation costs for the in-person workshop in Manchester are covered.

What is ELEAD?

ELEAD was a European leadership programme that successfully enabled women leaders across the ELIXIR life-science infrastructure. PeerLadder is the UK adaptation, extended to all underrepresented dRTP groups and centred on peer mentoring — the most impactful element of the original model.

What happens after the programme?

Participants will be supported with materials and guidance to continue as a self-organised peer group. Outcomes will feed into DisCouRSE, ELIXIR-UK, and SSI to shape inclusive leadership and EDI policy nationally.
