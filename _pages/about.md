            <div class="col-lg-7 col-md-12 about_intro_text">
                <p class="apply_lead">Peer Ladder is a peer-mentoring initiative that empowers underrepresented digital Research Technical Professionals (dRTPs) to climb the leadership ladder together – building confidence, community and inclusive leadership across UK research-performing organisations (RPOs).</p>
                <p>dRTPs are essential to data-driven research, yet many remain in supporting roles without clear routes into leadership. Women, disabled people, LGBTQIA+, and minority ethnic professionals are particularly under-represented at senior levels, limiting diversity of thought and innovation.</p>
                <p>Peer Ladder builds on a proven European model (ELEAD) that successfully enabled women leaders within the European ELIXIR life-science infrastructure. The UK pilot adapts this approach to all underrepresented dRTP groups, focusing on peer mentoring – the most impactful element of the original model.</p>
                <p>Participants receive structured peer support and training from Open Life Science (OLS), which provides onboarding and facilitation to create a safe, inclusive learning environment that builds allyship and leadership skills.</p>
                <p>As a test-pilot, Peer Ladder will demonstrate the value of this model nationally. Participants will be supported with materials and guidance to continue meeting as a self-organised group beyond the project. Outcomes will feed into DisCouRSE, ELIXIR-UK, and SSI to shape inclusive leadership and EDI policy across the digital research community.</p>
            </div>

                    <h3 class="pl_feature_title">What is a dRTP?</h3>
                    <p class="pl_feature_body">Digital Research Technical Professionals develop and maintain the digital infrastructure, software, data pipelines and computational tools that power modern research – including research software engineers, data stewards, bioinformaticians, and HPC specialists.</p>
                </div>
            </div>

            <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-6 mb-30">
                <div class="pl_feature_box">
                    <div class="pl_feature_icon"><i class="icon_group"></i></div>
                    <h3 class="pl_feature_title">Who can apply?</h3>
                    <p class="pl_feature_body">Anyone with at least three years' experience in a dRTP role at a UK research-performing organisation. PeerLadder particularly welcomes applications from women, disabled individuals, neurodivergent professionals, LGBTQIA+ practitioners, and those from minority ethnic or lower socioeconomic backgrounds.</p>
                </div>
            </div>

            <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-6 mb-30">
                <div class="pl_feature_box">
                    <div class="pl_feature_icon"><i class="icon_calendar"></i></div>
                    <h3 class="pl_feature_title">What is the time commitment?</h3>
                    <p class="pl_feature_body">Typically one to two hours per month outside of workshops. The programme includes a virtual onboarding, one in-person workshop in Manchester (travel fully covered), a systemic leadership workshop, and monthly virtual peer-mentoring sessions from August 2026 through March 2027.</p>
                </div>
            </div>

            <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-6 mb-30">
                <div class="pl_feature_box">
                    <div class="pl_feature_icon"><i class="icon_check_alt2"></i></div>
                    <h3 class="pl_feature_title">Is there a cost?</h3>
                    <p class="pl_feature_body">No. PeerLadder is fully funded by DisCouRSE. There is no application fee and no charge to participate. All reasonable travel and accommodation costs for the in-person workshop in Manchester are covered.</p>
                </div>
            </div>

            <div class="col-lg-4 col-md-6 mb-30">
